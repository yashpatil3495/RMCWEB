ABOUT US

The Mathematics Club was founded by two students whose curiosity led them to an interstate competition, where they represented Pune as the only engineering participants. Their experience and the appreciation received from the panel inspired the question, "Why not others?" Collaboration with faculty members during early event planning naturally evolved into the formal establishment of the club. Today, the club is dedicated exclusively to pure mathematics and guided by the motto "Apply and Innovate."

HISTORY OF MATHEMATICS

A lineage of brilliance — the Indian mathematicians who shaped how the world thinks.

476 – 550 CE
α

Aryabhata

Astronomy · Algebra

One of India's earliest mathematical giants. His Aryabhatiya computed π with remarkable precision, explained Earth's axial rotation, and developed methods for solving quadratic equations centuries before the West.

598 – 668 CE
0

Brahmagupta

Algebra · Number Theory

Author of the Brahmasphutasiddhanta, the first written text to treat zero as a number with defined arithmetic rules — a contribution that transformed all of mathematics.

1114 – 1185
∫

Bhaskara II

Calculus · Astronomy

Known as Bhaskaracharya, his Lilavati remains a landmark text. He explored early concepts of differential calculus and described gravitational attraction centuries before Newton.

1340 – 1425
π

Madhava of Sangamagrama

Infinite Series · Calculus

Founder of the Kerala School of Mathematics. He derived infinite series for sine, cosine, and π — anticipating the work of Newton and Leibniz by nearly 300 years.

1887 – 1920
∞

Srinivasa Ramanujan

Number Theory · Infinite Series

A self-taught genius from Tamil Nadu whose notebooks still yield undiscovered theorems. His collaboration with G. H. Hardy at Cambridge produced breakthroughs in number theory, continued fractions, and partition functions. National Mathematics Day is celebrated on his birthday — 22 December.

1920 – 2023
σ

C. R. Rao

Statistics · Information Theory

World-renowned statistician whose Cramér–Rao Bound set the fundamental limit on estimator precision. Recipient of India's Padma Vibhushan and the International Prize in Statistics.

1923 – 1983
Λ

Harish-Chandra

Representation Theory · Analysis

A titan of abstract algebra and harmonic analysis on Lie groups. His work reshaped modern mathematics, and the Harish-Chandra Research Institute in Prayagraj carries his legacy.

1929 – 2013
×

Shakuntala Devi

Mental Arithmetic · Education

Known globally as the "Human Computer." She multiplied two 13-digit numbers in 28 seconds and earned a Guinness World Record, dedicating her life to making mathematics joyful and accessible.

1974 – Present
ζ

Manjul Bhargava

Number Theory · Fields Medal

Princeton professor and Fields Medal laureate (2014). His revolutionary work on higher-order composition laws in number theory opened entirely new directions in one of mathematics' oldest fields.
